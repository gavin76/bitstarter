Kanji Study aims to bring Japanese web articles to you to help your immersion study and aid recognition of Japanese Kanji based on your JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test) level. It hopes to make learning Kanji more enjoyable and relevant through reading articles of interest to help with japanese comprehension.

Intermediate learners of Japanese have developed proficiency in reading hiragana and katakana, and further proficiency depends on recognition of kanji. With 1,945 officially endorsed kanji and approximately 2,500 commonly used in books and newspapers, and the various readings of kanji used in different situations, learning kanji is not easy but is essential.

How does Kanji Study help?

Kanji Study grabs articles from your favourite news/RSS feeds and displays them with furigana to enable easy phonetic reading of kanji and translations kanji based on your chosen JLPT level. This allows Japanese learners to focus on a limited batch of kanji characters for drilling and contextual comprehension. The aim would be to enable readers to enjoy more of the article and maintain interest in trying to read even though a complete understanding of the entire article is not possible. This simulates the reading-learning process that we have experienced as children when we pick up an interesting book, and try to read even though there may be many words we do not understand.

FAQ

How does it work?
Kanji Study is a web app where users select or enter a RSS feed source and the articles will be parsed for selected kanji translations based on a pre-selected JLPT level. The phonetic pronounciations in hiragana and the English translation will be shown to readers. It will essentially function as a Japanese-English optimized RSS reader.
Any other features will be implemented?
With time it is hoped Kanji Study will developed into a full-featured web based study tool including flashcard and quizzing to speed up learning and retention of Kanji.
What is the JLPT?
The JLPT stands for the Japanese-Language Proficiency Test. It has been offered by the Japan Foundation and Japan Educational Exchanges and Services since 1984 as areliable means of evlauating and certifying the Japanese proficiency of non-native speakers. Further information can be obtained at their official website.
